# Privacy Policy **App:** Common **Developer:** Victor Tarnovski **Effective date:** June 25, 2026 **Last updated:** June 25, 2026 **Contact:** common.ios.support@gmail.com This Privacy Policy explains what information the Common app (the "App," "we," "us," or "our") collects, how we use it, who we share it with, and the choices and rights you have. It applies to the Common iOS app and the backend service that supports it. We have built Common around a simple principle: **the content and information you provide belongs to you, not to us.** We store it only so the App can function, and we describe exactly how below. --- ## 1. Our privacy commitments - **We do not claim ownership of your content.** You keep all rights to the photos, captions, messages, and other content you create. We store and process it only to operate the App for you and the people you choose to share it with. - **We do not sell your personal information.** We have never sold it and will not sell, rent, or trade it to anyone. - **We do not build advertising or "social profiles" about you.** We do not use your data to profile you, track you across other apps or websites, or feed third‑party advertising or data‑broker systems. - **We collect only what the App needs to work.** We do not collect data we don't need to provide the service. We store the information you upload because it is technically necessary to run the App — for example, your posts must be saved so they can be shown in feeds, and your messages must be saved so they can be delivered. --- ## 2. Information we collect We only collect information that is needed to operate Common. We do **not** collect location data, advertising identifiers (IDFA), contacts, health data, financial/payment information, or browsing activity in other apps. We do not use third‑party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs. ### a. Information you provide when you create an account Common uses **Sign in with Apple** as its only sign‑in method. When you sign in, we receive and store: - A unique Apple account identifier (Apple's `user identifier` / "sub") used to recognize your account on return visits. - Your email address, **only if** Apple shares it. If you choose Apple's "Hide My Email" feature, we receive only a private relay address, not your real email. Apple may also withhold the email entirely. We do **not** receive or store your Apple password. ### b. Profile information - A username (one is auto‑generated for you at sign‑up; you may change it). - A display name. - An optional profile description / bio. - An optional profile picture. - Your account privacy setting (public or private). ### c. Content you create and upload - **Posts** — photos/media and an optional caption/description. - **Comments** you write on posts. - **Direct messages** and group chat messages, including chat names. - **Likes**, **follows / follow requests**, and **blocks**. - **Read receipts** indicating messages you have viewed. - **Reports** you submit about content (including the reason you provide), used for safety and content moderation. ### d. Authentication and technical data - Session tokens (a short‑lived access token and a refresh token) used to keep you signed in securely. - Timestamps recording when items are created or updated. We do not collect device advertising identifiers, precise or coarse location, or analytics telemetry. --- ## 3. How we use your information We use the information above **only** to operate and support the App, specifically to: - Create and maintain your account and keep you securely signed in. - Display your profile, posts, comments, likes, and follower relationships to you and to other users according to your privacy settings. - Deliver and display your messages to the people in your conversations. - Enforce privacy settings, follow approvals, and blocks. - Review reported content and keep the community safe. - Maintain the security, integrity, and reliability of the service, and to prevent abuse, fraud, and technical problems. - Comply with legal obligations where required. We do **not** use your information for advertising, for sale to third parties, or to build behavioral or social‑graph profiles for any purpose other than the core social features you are using directly (for example, showing you posts from people you follow). --- ## 4. How your information is shared ### Other users Common is a social app, so some information is shared with other users by design: - Your username, display name, bio, and profile picture are visible to other users. - Your posts, comments, and likes are visible according to your account privacy setting (public accounts are visible to anyone in the App; private accounts are visible only to approved followers). - Your messages are visible to the other participants in that conversation. ### Service providers We use a small number of service providers strictly to run the App. They may process data only on our behalf and only to provide their service to us: - **Apple** — for Sign in with Apple authentication. See Apple's Privacy Policy at https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/. - **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** — our application server and PostgreSQL database run on AWS infrastructure in the US East (N. Virginia) region. AWS stores and processes the App's data on our behalf as our hosting provider. See the AWS Privacy Notice at https://aws.amazon.com/privacy/. We do not authorize these providers to use your information for their own purposes. ### Legal We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or other valid legal process, or where necessary to protect the rights, safety, or property of our users or the public. ### We do not sell your data We do not and will not sell your personal information, and we do not share it with advertisers or data brokers. --- ## 5. Data retention We keep your information for as long as your account is active, because it is needed to operate the App (for example, your posts and messages must remain stored to be displayed and delivered). When you delete your account, we delete your account and the personal content associated with it from our active systems. Some content that is inherently shared may persist in limited ways — for example, a message you sent remains visible to its other recipient(s) as part of their conversation, and reports may be retained for safety and legal record‑keeping. Residual copies may remain in encrypted backups for a limited period before being overwritten. --- ## 6. Your rights and choices You have control over your information: - **Access and update** — You can view and edit your profile information (username, name, bio, picture) and privacy setting directly in the App. - **Delete content** — You can delete your posts, comments, and other content you have created at any time within the App. - **Delete your account** — You can permanently delete your account and its associated personal data from within the App (see Section 7). - **Revoke Sign in with Apple** — You can stop the App's access to your Apple account at any time on your device under **Settings → [your name] → Sign in with Apple → Common → Stop Using Apple ID.** Depending on where you live (for example, the EU/EEA, UK, or California), you may have additional rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, or port your personal information, and to object to certain processing. To exercise these rights, contact us at common.ios.support@gmail.com. We do not discriminate against you for exercising your rights. --- ## 7. Account and data deletion You can permanently delete your account from within the App at any time. When you delete your account: - Your profile, posts, comments, likes, follow relationships, and other personal content are removed from our active systems. - Exceptions described in Section 5 (Data retention) may apply. If you are unable to access the App and wish to delete your account, contact us at common.ios.support@gmail.com and we will process your request. --- ## 8. Data security We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including encrypted connections (HTTPS) between the App and our servers, secure on‑device storage of authentication tokens in the iOS Keychain, and access controls on our backend. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security, but we work to protect your information and to limit access to it. --- ## 9. Children's privacy Common is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or the minimum age required in your country), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at common.ios.support@gmail.com and we will delete it. --- ## 10. International data transfers Your information is stored and processed on Amazon Web Services in the United States (US East / N. Virginia region). If you access the App from outside the United States, you understand that your information will be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States, which may have data‑protection laws different from those in your country. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards for such transfers. --- ## 11. Changes to this policy We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, notify you within the App. Your continued use of the App after an update means you accept the revised policy. --- ## 12.
